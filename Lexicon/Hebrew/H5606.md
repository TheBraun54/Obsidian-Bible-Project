# Lexicon Entry H5606

**Lemma**: סָפַק

**Transliteration (xlit)**: çâphaq

**Pronunciation**: saw-fak'

**Description**:
or שָׂפַק; (1 Kings 20:10; Job 27:23; Isaiah 2:6), a primitive root; to clap the hands (in token of compact, derision, grief, indignation, or punishment); by implication of satisfaction, to be enough; by implication of excess, to vomit; clap, smite, strike, suffice, wallow.
